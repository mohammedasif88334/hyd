1. Bonalu is a traditional Telangana festival celebrated mainly in Hyderabad to thank Goddess Mahakali for protecting people.
  2. It takes place in July-August (Ashada month), especially at temples like Golconda, Secunderabad, and Lal Darwaza.
  3. Women carry decorated pots (bonam) with rice and jaggery on their heads and offer them at temples.
  4. The festival includes colorful processions, folk dances, and the unique Pothuraju (a dancing male devotee).
  5. It is officially declared the state festival of Telangana and is celebrated with great enthusiasm and community spirit.
  1. Ramzan (Ramadan) is a holy month for Muslims, marked by fasting from dawn to sunset.
  2. In Hyderabad, it is celebrated with special prayers (Taraweeh), charity, and spiritual gatherings in mosques.
  3. The old city, especially Charminar area, becomes lively with night bazaars selling clothes, bangles, perfumes, and traditional foods.
  4. Famous dishes like haleem, kebabs, and sheer khurma are widely enjoyed during iftar (meal to break the fast).
  5. The festival ends with Eid-ul-Fitr, when people wear new clothes, offer Eid prayers, exchange gifts, and celebrate with family and friends.
